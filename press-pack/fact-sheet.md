## TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS

### Building Efficiency

**Waste Heat Recovery:**
- Air conditioner condenser heat → Hot water pre-heating
- Refrigerator waste heat → Space heating (winter)
- Clothes dryer exhaust → Ventilation air pre-heating
- Greywater heat recovery → Heat pump source elevation

**Water Multi-Cycling:**
1. Shower/basin greywater → Washing machine pre-rinse
2. Washing machine greywater → Toilet flushing
3. Kitchen sink (low-grease) → Garden irrigation
4. All greywater → Heat recovery before discharge

**Target Performance:**
- Minimum 7-star NatHERS rating
- 40% reduction in energy intensity (MJ/m²/year)
- 60% reduction in potable water use

### Electric Mobility

**Infrastructure Density:**
- Battery swapping stations: 1 per 5,000 residents (urban)
- E-bike protected lanes: Every 500m in urban cores
- Fast charging points: Every 50km on major highways

**Battery Swapping:**
- Swap time: <3 minutes (Janus Electric standard)
- Battery formats: Standardised for interoperability
- V2G capability: Mandatory for all stations
- Grid services: Frequency control, peak shaving

**E-Bike Network:**
- Protected lanes with physical separation
- Integration with public transport hubs
- Battery swapping at major stations
- Target: 40% of urban trips by 2035

### Circular Economy

**Battery Recycling:**
- Collection: Mandatory take-back at all retail points
- Processing: Regional microfactories (SMaRT Centre model)
- Recovery rate: 95% of materials
- Outputs:
  - Lithium carbonate → New battery production
  - Steel casings → Construction materials
  - Graphite → Soil amendment or battery anodes

**Vape Battery Stream:**
- Current: B-cycle scheme acceptance
- Proposed: Co-location at all swapping stations
- Processing: Thermal transformation (Sahajwalla method)
- Products: Powerbanks, battery materials

**Construction Waste:**
- On-site microfactories for concrete recycling
- E-waste → 3D printing filament
- Plastics → Modular building components
- Target: 80% diversion from landfill

## IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE

### Phase 1: Foundation (0-12 months)
- **Month 1-3:** Establish Battery Stewardship Authority
- **Month 3-6:** Amend National Construction Code
- **Month 6-12:** Launch $500M EV Infrastructure Fund
- **Budget:** $750M

### Phase 2: Scale-Up (1-3 years)
- **Year 1-2:** Urban planning standards implementation
- **Year 2-3:** Road user charging pilot programs
- **Year 1-3:** TAFE program expansion (10,000 trainees)
- **Budget:** $1.2B

### Phase 3: Transformation (3-10 years)
- **Year 3-5:** Net-zero building mandate
- **Year 5-7:** 95% battery recovery achievement
- **Year 7-10:** 40% active transport mode share
- **Budget:** $2.5B

**Total Investment: $4.45B over 10 years**

## GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

**National Energy Transition Steering Committee**
- Chair: Prime Minister or Treasurer
- Members: Ministers for Energy, Transport, Environment, Industry
- Reporting: Quarterly to Parliament

**Three Implementation Bodies:**

1. **Building Efficiency Taskforce**
   - Led by Department of Climate Change
   - Industry representatives
   - Consumer advocates

2. **Electric Mobility Authority**
   - Led by Department of Infrastructure
   - State/territory representatives
   - EV industry stakeholders

3. **Circular Economy Centre**
   - Led by Department of Industry
   - Research institutions (UNSW SMaRT Centre)
   - Waste industry representatives

**Oversight:**
- Real-time public dashboard
- Annual independent audit
- Parliamentary review every 2 years
